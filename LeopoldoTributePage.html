Leopoldo López

A Hero behind bars.

Leopoldo Lopez speaking to a crowd of supporters.

Here's a time line of Leopoldo Lopez's life:

  • 1971, April 29th: Born in Caracas, Venezuela
  • 1993: Graduates from Kenyon College in Gambier, Ohio receiving a bachelor's degree in Economics and Sociology.
  • 1996: Graduates from Harvard University's Kennedy School of Government receiving a Master's degree of Public Policy
  • 1996-1999: Starts working as an analyst and consultant, and later becomes an analyst for the chief economist to the Planning Vice-President in Petróleos de Venezuela S.A. (PDVSA). He also worked as a professor of Institutional Economy in the Economics Department at Universidad Católica Andrés Bello.
  • 2000: Is elected Mayor of Chacao by 51% of the vote.
  • 2004: Is re-elected Mayor of Chacao by 81% of the vote. At the end of his second term as mayor, his approval was of 92%.
  • 2007: Marries Lilian Tintori and receives the Kenyon College Honoris Causa Doctorate in Law.
  • 2007-2008: Recieves the Premio Transparencia Award, to the most transparent city mayor of Venezuela, granted by the Venezuela branch of Transparency International.
  • 2008: Wins Third place, in the World Mayors contest which nominates the "world's most outstanding mayors". However, in Venezuela, He was falsely accused of corruption and was banned from running in the November 2008 Regional Elections. López brought his case to many instances including the the Inter-American Commission on Human Rights (IACHR), The Human Rights Commission of the international Mercosur Parliament, The Supreme Tribunal in Venezuela, The Organization of American States, and others.
  • 2009: Wins Most Innovative People Award for Resiliancy from the Future Capitals World Summit and creates his own political party "Voluntad Popular".
  • 2011: Was cleared of all charges.
  • 2013: According to the Global Misery Index Venezuela ranked as the top spot globally with the highest misery index score.[1]
  • 2014: Venezuela enters an economic recession having its GDP growth decline to -3.0%[2] Which motivates protests against the governement leading to:
      - January: Calls for a non-violent demostration against government-sponsored violence toward non-conformists.
      - February 12th: A group of government supporters violently infiltrate the peaceful demostration summoned by him and several protesters are shot and killed.
      - February 13th: A Warrant of Arrest is issued chargning him with arson, murder, terrorism and conspiracy.
      - February 16th: Via a YouTube video he summons another peaceful march for the 18th of February and states: "I have nothing to fear, I have not committed any crime. If there is some decision to illegally arrest me, well, that is where I will be".
      - February 18th: In presence of thousands of supporters He turns himself in, and as the Venezuelan National Guard arrests him he says: "If my imprisonment helps awaken our people... then this infamous imprisonment that Nicolás Maduro wants so openly and cowardly, then for me it will have been worth it". He encourages Venezuelans "to embrace our right to protest. But do so peacefully and without resorting to violence."

"If the sentence condemns me you will be more fearful to read it than I will be to hear it, because you know that I'm innocent".

— López in pre-sentencing speech, 2015
